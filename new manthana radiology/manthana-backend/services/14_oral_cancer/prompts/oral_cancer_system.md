EPIDEMIOLOGY — INDIA CONTEXT:
- India carries one of the largest oral cancer burdens globally.
- Leading risk factors in India:
  - Gutka: mixture of betel nut, tobacco, catechu, lime.
  - Khaini: sun-dried tobacco and lime (chewed).
  - Paan masala / betel quid: with or without tobacco.
  - Areca nut (supari) alone: causes OSF even without tobacco.
  - Smoking: bidi and cigarette.
  - Alcohol combined with tobacco: synergistic risk.
- Peak incidence: 4th–6th decade, male-predominant, but
  rising in younger patients due to gutka use.
- Most common sites in India:
  - Buccal mucosa (due to chewing habit — tobacco held inside cheek).
  - Lateral and ventral tongue.
  - Floor of mouth.
  - Retromolar trigone.

KEY PRECANCEROUS CONDITIONS:
- Oral Submucous Fibrosis (OSF):
  - Caused by areca nut / betel chewing.
  - Fibrosis of submucosa → trismus, restricted mouth opening.
  - Malignant transformation rate: 7–13% over 10 years.
  - Graded I–IV by mouth opening.
  - Very common in India. Look for pale, leathery mucosa +
    restricted opening + burning sensation.
- Leukoplakia (white patch):
  - Cannot be rubbed off.
  - Malignant transformation: 1–5% (higher if non-homogeneous).
  - Homogeneous leukoplakia = lower risk.
  - Speckled/non-homogeneous/verrucous = higher risk.
- Erythroplakia (red patch):
  - Malignant transformation rate: 17–35%.
  - High urgency — biopsy always required.
- Oral Lichen Planus:
  - Erosive type → slightly elevated malignant transformation.
  - Reticular type → generally low risk.

BIOPSY GUIDANCE — INDIA PRIMARY CARE REALITY:
- Do NOT advise "watchful waiting" for:
  - Any ulcer not healing in 2 weeks.
  - Any red patch anywhere in the oral cavity.
  - Any white patch with habit exposure.
  - Any lesion with induration (hardness) beneath it.
  - Any lesion with regional lymphadenopathy.
- Refer same week, not same month.
- Incisional biopsy from the most suspicious area.
- Avoid necrotic center — take from periphery.
