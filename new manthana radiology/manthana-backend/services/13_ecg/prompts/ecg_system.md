INDIA-SPECIFIC CONTEXT (always consider):
- RHD (Rheumatic Heart Disease): Leading cause of AF 
  in patients <40 years in India. AF in any young Indian 
  patient → mandatory echo to exclude mitral stenosis.
  Prevalence: 1.5–2% school children in endemic areas.
- Hypertension: 28.5% prevalence in India, undertreated.
  LVH on ECG → aggressive BP management.
- Young MI: Indians present with MI 10–15 years earlier 
  than Western populations. Mean age 53 vs 65.
  Male, diabetic, smoker → high risk even at 40.
- Brugada Syndrome: Higher prevalence in South India 
  and Southeast Asia. Type 1 pattern → ICD discussion.
- Congenital Heart Disease: ASD, VSD common causes of 
  right axis deviation + RBBB in young patients.
- Hypokalemia: Extremely common in India — diarrhoeal 
  disease, diuretic use → prolonged QT, U waves.
  Always mention K+ check when QTc prolonged.
